FLAN RECIPES --- STÉPHANE GLACIER QUENTIN LECHAT

Flan Cake | Stéphane Glacier Style

Ingredients

  1. 750g of whole milk
  2. 250g of sugar
  3. 60g of egg yolk (4 yolks)
  4. 150g of egg (3 eggs)
  5. 250g of milk cream
  6. 80g of corn starch or cream powder
  7. 1 tablespoon of vanilla essence or one vanilla bean

Preparation

1. In a saucepan, combine the milk, half of the sugar, all the cream, and the vanilla extract. Heat over medium heat, stirring to dissolve the ingredients.

2. Turn off the heat when the mixture starts to boil and let it stand.

3. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigarate for 2 hours.

4. Bring the milk mixture back to a boil. Gradually add half of it to the egg mixture, mixing well. Once combined, pour the egg mixture back into the remaining milk, stirring constantly. Remove from the heat and continue to stir until it thickens. Return the mixture to the heat and stir vigorously for 2 minutes after it starts to boil.

5. Pour the mixture into the mold and smooth the surface with a spatula. Let it cool and refrigerate for 4 hours.

6. Preheat the oven to 160°C (320°F). Bake the flan for about 1 hour and 15 minutes to 1 hour and 30 minutes, rotating the mold occasionally to ensure even cooking.

7. Apply syrup to the flan to give it a glossy finish and sprinkle powdered sugar on the dough for decoration.

8. Consume it after preserving it in the refrigerator overnight.

Flan Cake | Quentin Lechat Style

Ingredients

  1. 715g of whole milk
  2. 155g of sugar
  3. 165g of egg yolk (8-9 yolks)
  4. 200g of milk cream
  5. 60g of corn starch or cream powder
  6. 100g of butter
  7. 1 tablespoon of vanilla essence or one vanilla bean

Preparation

1. In a saucepan, add all the milk (including the vanilla pod if using) and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and add the vanilla essence.

2. In a bowl, mix 155g of sugar with all the egg yolks. Add the cornstarch or cream powder and mix until homogeneous.

3. Return the milk to the heat. When it boils, add half of it to the egg yolk mixture, stirring continuously. Then pour this mixture back into the boiling milk, continuing to stir constantly.

4. Once combined, remove from heat and stir until thickened. Return to medium heat and stir until it boils again, then mix vigorously for 2 minutes.

5. Remove from heat and add 100g of cold diced butter and 200ml of cream. Mix thoroughly and pour over the dough, smoothing the surface with a spatula. Let it cool in the refrigerator for 4 hours.

6. Preheat the oven to 165°C (329°F). Bake the flan for 1 hour to 1 hour and 15 minutes, rotating occasionally to ensure even cooking.

7. Cover the flan with syrup and sprinkle powdered sugar on the dough. Refrigerate until the next day and serve cold.
